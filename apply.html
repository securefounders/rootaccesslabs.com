Apply to RootAccess Labs

Before You Apply

RootAccess Labs admits a limited number of learners per cohort.

This application is not a formality. It helps us assess whether the program is a good fit — for you and for us.

Not all applicants are accepted.

What RootAccess Labs Is Looking For

We are not looking for perfect backgrounds. We are looking for intent, effort, and seriousness.

You are encouraged to apply if you:

  • Are genuinely interested in cybersecurity as a discipline
  • Want to understand how attacks and defenses actually work
  • Are willing to put in consistent effort outside live sessions
  • Are comfortable with challenge, failure, and feedback
  • Prefer depth and understanding over shortcuts

Who Should Not Apply

Please do not apply if you:

  • Are looking for quick certificates or easy credentials
  • Expect guaranteed placements, referrals, or outcomes
  • Want purely recorded or passive learning
  • Are exploring casually without clear intent

The programs are deliberately demanding.

Programs You Can Apply For

You may apply to one of the following tracks:

  • SOC & Blue Team Operations (2 months)
  • Ethical Hacking (3 months)
  • Vulnerability Assessment & Penetration Testing – VAPT (3 months)
  • Combined Track: Ethical Hacking + VAPT (4 months)

Final placement into a program may depend on your background and readiness.

How the Application Process Works

1

Submit Your Application

Provide honest and thoughtful responses. There are no "right" answers.

2

Review

Applications are reviewed manually. This may take a few days.

3

Conversation (If Required)

Some applicants may be invited for a short discussion to assess fit.

4

Decision

You will be informed whether you are accepted, deferred, or not a fit at this time.

What the Application Will Ask

Basic Information

  • Name
  • Email
  • Location
  • Current role or student status

Background

  • Education or work experience
  • Prior exposure to cybersecurity (if any)

Program Interest

  • Program you are applying for
  • Why you chose this program

Intent & Commitment

  • Why you want to learn cybersecurity
  • Why RootAccess Labs specifically
  • Time you can realistically commit each week

Optional (Strongly Considered)

  • GitHub, labs, or portfolio (if any)
  • Prior projects or independent learning efforts

Important Notes

• Admission is selective

• Effort matters more than background

• There are no guaranteed outcomes

• You are expected to practice outside live sessions

If these expectations feel uncomfortable, this may not be the right program.

After You Apply

If accepted, you will receive:

  • Program details
  • Onboarding instructions
  • Schedule and expectations

If not accepted, you may be encouraged to prepare further and reapply later.

Cybersecurity is not learned passively.

It is built through effort, curiosity, and discipline.

If that resonates with you, apply to RootAccess Labs.
